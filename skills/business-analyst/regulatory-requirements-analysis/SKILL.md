# Regulatory Requirements Analysis

## Purpose

Help teams understand how external or internal obligations affect business requirements and change scope.

## Use When

- A regulation, policy, audit finding, or compliance rule affects the product or process.
- Legal or regulatory wording must become business rules and requirements.
- Approval, traceability, or evidence requirements are important.

## Inputs

- Regulation, policy, audit item, legal note, control requirement, or compliance interpretation.
- Business process, affected stakeholders, systems, data, and documents.
- Accountable legal, compliance, or business owner.

## Workflow

1. Identify obligation, source, accountable owner, affected scope, and effective dates.
2. Translate obligations into business rules, requirements, controls, and evidence needs.
3. Separate interpretation questions from implementation questions.
4. Capture traceability from source obligation to requirement and acceptance condition.
5. Hand off legal interpretation, technical specification, QA strategy, or operational control ownership as needed.

## Outputs

- Regulatory requirement brief.
- Obligation-to-requirement traceability notes.
- Business rules and acceptance implications.
- Owner questions and handoff list.

## Boundaries

- Does not provide legal advice or final legal interpretation.
- Does not own audit or compliance sign-off.
- Does not define technical implementation details.
