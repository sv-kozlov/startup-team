# Business Case Analysis

## Purpose

Frame why a change should be considered and what business tradeoffs, benefits, costs, risks, and assumptions matter for decision-making.

## Use When

- Stakeholders need a decision brief before committing to scope.
- Options must be compared by value, risk, cost, effort, or constraints.
- Benefits and assumptions need to be explicit before requirements work.

## Inputs

- Business problem, objectives, options, constraints, rough costs, risks, stakeholders, and evidence.
- Product or delivery context where available.
- Known decision criteria.

## Workflow

1. State problem, desired outcome, decision, and options.
2. Capture expected benefits, costs, risks, dependencies, and assumptions.
3. Identify measurable success signals and validation needs.
4. Separate business case from product priority and technical solution.
5. Hand off prioritization to Product Manager and estimation to Tech Lead or Engineering.

## Outputs

- Business case brief.
- Option comparison.
- Benefit, cost, risk, and assumption list.
- Decision-needed notes.

## Boundaries

- Does not approve investment, budget, or roadmap priority.
- Does not create financial model beyond available evidence.
- Does not own delivery estimate or technical feasibility assessment.
