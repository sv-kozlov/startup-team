# Business Problem Framing

## Purpose

Turn an ambiguous request into a clear business problem, target outcome, context, constraints, assumptions, and decision question.

## Use When

- A request describes a desired solution but not the business problem.
- Causes, symptoms, goals, or success signals are mixed.
- Stakeholders need a shared problem statement before scope or requirements.

## Inputs

- Business request, incident pattern, initiative idea, complaint, regulation, or opportunity.
- Current process, user pain, cost, risk, revenue, compliance, or service-quality context.
- Available evidence and stakeholder assumptions.

## Workflow

1. Separate symptom, root cause hypothesis, business need, target outcome, and proposed solution.
2. Ask why the change matters and who is affected.
3. Capture current-state evidence and missing validation.
4. Define success signals in business terms, not implementation terms.
5. State assumptions, constraints, exclusions, and decisions needed.

## Outputs

- Business problem statement.
- Goal and success-signal brief.
- Root-cause hypothesis list.
- Assumptions, constraints, and decision-needed list.

## Boundaries

- Does not make final product priority decisions.
- Does not produce technical solution design.
- Does not claim causal proof without evidence.
