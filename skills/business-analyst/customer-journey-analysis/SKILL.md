# Customer Journey Analysis

## Purpose

Connect user experience, business process, touchpoints, and operational constraints so requirements reflect the actual journey.

## Use When

- A product or process change affects user journey across steps, teams, or channels.
- Pain points, service gaps, or handoffs must be mapped.
- UX/UI or Product Manager needs business context for journey decisions.

## Inputs

- User goals, journey notes, process steps, channels, support issues, research notes, or stakeholder examples.
- Business rules, constraints, systems, and ownership points.
- Known metrics or evidence where available.

## Workflow

1. Define user segment, goal, trigger, start, end, and channels.
2. Map stages, touchpoints, actions, pain points, emotions or friction, and business owners.
3. Link journey steps to business process, rules, data, and system dependencies.
4. Identify opportunity areas, risks, and validation needs.
5. Hand off UX research and interaction design to UX/UI.

## Outputs

- Journey map brief.
- Touchpoint and pain-point list.
- Process and ownership links.
- Requirements and handoff notes.

## Boundaries

- Does not own UX research plan or visual journey artifact design.
- Does not make final product priority decisions.
- Does not define implementation details.
