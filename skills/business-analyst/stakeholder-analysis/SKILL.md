# Stakeholder Analysis

## Purpose

Identify who is affected by a change, who provides requirements, who decides, and whose constraints must be reflected in business analysis artifacts.

## Use When

- Stakeholders, users, decision owners, or approvers are unclear.
- Requirements conflict across groups.
- Interviews, workshops, approvals, or handoffs must be planned.

## Inputs

- Business goal, initiative, product area, or process.
- Known teams, users, owners, policies, and systems.
- Existing requirements, incidents, requests, or documents.

## Workflow

1. List stakeholder groups and their relationship to the change.
2. Classify each group by decision power, expertise, impact, and dependency.
3. Identify missing voices, conflicts, approval owners, and escalation paths.
4. Record what each stakeholder provides: goals, rules, constraints, data, acceptance, or sign-off.
5. Convert the map into interview questions, workshop invitees, and handoff targets.

## Outputs

- Stakeholder map.
- Decision and approval owner notes.
- Interview or workshop plan.
- Conflict and missing-input list.

## Boundaries

- Does not own organization design or staffing decisions.
- Does not replace Product Manager ownership of product priority.
- Does not replace Product Manager and Tech Lead ownership of startup delivery commitments, or Delivery ownership when that optional role exists.
