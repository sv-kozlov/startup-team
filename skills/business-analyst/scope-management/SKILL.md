# Scope Management

## Purpose

Make the business scope explicit enough for product decisions, system specification, estimation, and delivery planning.

## Use When

- Scope is unclear, expanding, or mixed with future ideas.
- An initiative needs inclusions, exclusions, dependencies, and assumptions.
- Backlog items need readiness before handoff.

## Inputs

- Business goal, feature request, process, stakeholder expectations, constraints, and dependencies.
- Existing backlog items, roadmap context, requirements, or open questions.
- Known deadlines, risks, and decision owners.

## Workflow

1. Define objective, in-scope outcomes, out-of-scope items, and non-goals.
2. Separate must-have business capability from optional improvement and future phase.
3. Identify dependencies, assumptions, risks, and decisions needed.
4. Check readiness against clarity, value, acceptance, feasibility signals, and ownership.
5. Hand off priority decisions to Product Manager and technical decomposition to System Analyst or Engineering.

## Outputs

- Scope statement.
- In/out list.
- Dependency and assumption list.
- Readiness review and handoff notes.

## Boundaries

- Does not own final product priority or delivery commitment.
- Does not own technical task decomposition.
- Does not commit resources or timeline.
