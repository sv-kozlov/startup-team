## Priority 1 — Canonical Method References

Authoritative primary sources used as the basis for skill workflows and patterns:

- **Scrum Guide** (Schwaber & Sutherland, 2020): https://scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html
  — Backlog ownership, sprint goal, sprint ceremonies, DoD, transparency principles.
- **Marty Cagan, "Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love"** (2nd ed., SVPG Press, 2017)
  — Product ownership, MVP, scope and delivery feedback loop.
- **Roman Pichler, "Strategize: Product Strategy and Product Roadmap Practices"**: https://www.romanpichler.com/books/strategize/
  — Backlog management, sprint goal, product-side delivery discipline.
- **Mike Cohn, "User Stories Applied: For Agile Software Development"** (Addison-Wesley, 2004)
  — User story format, acceptance criteria, splitting heuristics, DoR patterns.
- **Jeff Patton, "User Story Mapping: Discover the Whole Story, Build the Right Product"** (O'Reilly, 2014)
  — Story mapping, scope slicing, release planning, MVP definition.

## Priority 3 — Supplementary and Context Reading

Additional sources for extended practice and domain context:

  — Real-world product ownership, scope discipline, and delivery insights from Marty Cagan and team.
- **Roman Pichler Blog**: https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/
